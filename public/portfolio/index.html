Field Notes

Observations from 13+ years in freight & logistics operations

2026

Why Excel Still Beats Most TMS UIs

After working with McLeod, Turvo, and DAT daily, I've noticed something: dispatchers still default to Excel for ad-hoc analysis. The reason isn't nostalgia — it's flexibility. A good TMS handles structured workflows, but nothing beats a spreadsheet for "what-if" rate modeling and quick pivot analysis.

2025

The 12x Faster Detection Metric — What It Really Means

When I report "12x faster exception detection," it's not a vanity metric. It means catching a delayed shipment at hour 2 instead of hour 24 — the difference between a recovery plan and a customer escalation. Real-time tracking isn't about dashboards; it's about shrinking the gap between problem and awareness.

2024

Building PWAs for Warehouse Environments

Warehouses are hostile to web apps: spotty WiFi, Android tablets, no IT support. That's why I built the Inventory PWA offline-first. The lesson: don't assume connectivity. Design for the worst network condition, and everything else becomes a bonus.
